# Player Profile: Tom Barlow

## Quick Profile

- **Name:** Tom Barlow
- **Position:** Forward / Striker
- **Current Club:** FC Cincinnati
- **Number:** #16
- **Age:** 30 (Born July 8, 1995)
- **Height:** 6'2"
- **Hometown:** St. Louis, Missouri
- **Previous Clubs:** Chicago Fire, New York Red Bulls (RBNY)

## Transfer Details

- **Date Joined:** January 12, 2026
- **Transaction Type:** Free Agent Signing
- **Contract Terms:** Signed through **December 2027** with a club option for 2028.
- **Role:** Squad depth, attacking competition, physical target man.
- **Management Quote:** GM Chris Albright praised Barlow's "wealth of experience," "high character," and "skillset" as excellent additions to the attack.

## Career History & Stats

### Chicago Fire (2024–2025)

- **Appearances:** 50 (MLS Regular Season)
- **Goals:** 5
- **Key Moment:** Scored a hat trick against D.C. United in a 7-1 victory.
- **Summary:** Primarily used as a rotational forward/substitute. His option was declined following the 2025 season.

### New York Red Bulls (2019–2023)

- **Appearances:** 140 (All competitions)
- **Goals:** 18
- **Playoff Impact:** Scored 3 goals in MLS Cup Playoff matches.
- **Style:** Developed in the Red Bulls' high-pressing system; known for work rate and aerial ability.
- **USL Success:** Was a prolific scorer for NYRB II, netting 21 goals in 42 games (2018–2019).

### Collegiate Career

- **University:** University of Wisconsin
- **Stats:** 23 goals in 78 matches.
- **Draft:** Selected 39th overall in the 2018 MLS SuperDraft.

## Key Connections & Trivia

- **The Noonan Connection:** Barlow played youth soccer for **St. Louis Scott Gallagher**, the exact same youth club where FC Cincinnati Head Coach **Pat Noonan** developed. This shared background suggests a potential alignment in footballing culture and mentality.

## Summary for Presentation

Tom Barlow enters FC Cincinnati as a veteran MLS presence (30 years old) looking to bolster the attacking options. He is a 6'2" physical forward known more for his hold-up play, pressing work rate, and aerial threat than for high-volume goal scoring.

Averaging roughly a goal every 4-5 starts historically, he provides a different tactical option compared to other strikers. He arrives with significant experience in the Eastern Conference (RBNY, Chicago) and is signed on a team-friendly deal through 2027.
